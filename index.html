Abhyudaya is a unique pedagogic innovation which aims to address the 'Big Problem' of urban poverty & foster social responsibility & character building among B-school students through 'experiential learning'. SPJIMR is the first and may be the only B-school in India to launch a mentorship program aimed at preparing its participants for top management roles as part of the curriculum. It aims at recognizing the inequity (and perhaps injustice) existing in the wide gap between poor and rich of urban India and learn to contribute as a member of the 'have' group as meeting obligation to the 'have not' group and experience the joy of giving. Abhyudaya also gives them opportunity to appreciate the dynamics of urban poverty manifested through slums and identify the role and the challenges offered to the business sector.

Abhyudaya, a Sanskrit word, means "welfare and development for all". It is a 1.5 credit, year-long mentorship program through which first year MBA students mentor bright, underprivileged children (whom we call "Sitaras") of the neighbouring government schools. Through this initiative, SPJIMR is making an attempt to play the role of a catalyst in providing financial and other support to enable the Sitaras to realize their full potential. Beginning in 2008, this project is conceptualized and is being guided by the Honorary Dean Dr. M. L. Shrikant.

GMAC MET Fund 2012; USA, the body which holds the GMAT examination, has recognized Abhyudaya with the "Big Idea to Solve Big Problem" Award. Abhyudaya is one of the 12 awardees in the world and the only awardee from South East Asia. It has yet another feather in its cap. Abhyudaya, has won the jury award at the World Education Summit 2013 in the "Innovation by Management Institute" category.
